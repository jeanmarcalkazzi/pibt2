Overview

The Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF) problem consists in all agents having to move to their own destinations while avoiding collisions. In practical applications to the problem, such as for navigation in an automated warehouse, MAPF must be solved iteratively in a lifelong and online manner, ruling out any simple adaptation of offline and computationally-intensive optimal approaches. A challenge is thus to design sub-optimal and scalable algorithms with acceptable solution quality and predictable computational time that works even in lifelong situations.

To this end, we present a novel approach to iterative MAPF, that we call Priority Inheritance with Backtracking (PIBT). PIBT focuses on the adjacent movements of multiple agents relying on a simple mechanism, and hence can be applied to many domains. We prove that, regardless of their number, all agents are guaranteed to reach their destination within finite time, when the environment is a graph such that all pairs of adjacent nodes belong to a simple cycle (e.g., biconnected). Extensive experimental results over various scenarios, including real robot demon- strations, confirm that PIBT is adequate both for finding paths in large environments with many agents, as well as for conveying packages in an automated warehouse.
